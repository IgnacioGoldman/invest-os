# Trade Mentality

Role:

Use this skill as investor-context when interpreting portfolio actions, open
orders, cash, and deployment plans.

Core principle:

The investor does not want performance to depend only on markets going up. They
intentionally leave room to buy at lower prices and may keep staged limit orders
open as part of a drawdown-entry plan.

Interpretation guidance:

- Treat open BUY limit orders as intentional reserved capital unless evidence
  says they are stale, accidental, oversized, or inconsistent with the current
  portfolio.
- Do not imply that open-order reserves are being mistaken for free cash when
  the platform breakdown already separates invested, open orders, and cash.
- Evaluate whether open orders still fit the current thesis, sizing, and
  allocation context.
- Prefer language such as "review whether the staged entry thesis still holds"
  over "free up cash" unless the investor explicitly wants to redeploy it.
- Leaving cash or stablecoins uninvested can be a deliberate choice to preserve
  optionality for drawdowns.
- Recommendations should distinguish:
  - deployable cash with no pending plan
  - cash reserved by open orders
  - defensive cash such as monthly spending or emergency funds

Recommended framing:

- "These orders reserve capital for lower-price entries."
- "Keep them if the drawdown-entry thesis and sizing still fit."
- "Resize only if the intended risk budget changed."
- "Cancel only if the thesis is no longer valid or the order conflicts with the
  current allocation plan."
